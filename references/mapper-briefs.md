# Mapper Briefs

Mappers independently explore the option space. Use a small set of distinct briefs rather than many overlapping ones.

## Practical Mapper

Find the strongest conventional answer. Optimize for feasibility, clarity, and low coordination cost.

## High-Variance Mapper

Look for unusual, ambitious, or counterintuitive options. Optimize for upside and neglected possibilities.

## Constraint Mapper

Treat the hardest constraint as central. Design options that work under tight time, budget, attention, technical, or social limits.

## Stakeholder Mapper

Explore how different affected groups would judge the options. Surface incentives, objections, and adoption risks.

## Failure-Mode Mapper

Start from likely ways the effort could fail, then design options that avoid or survive those failures.

## Analogy Mapper

Import patterns from another domain such as games, biology, architecture, logistics, education, performance, or rituals.

## Output Contract

Each mapper should return:

- One to five candidate options.
- Strengths and weaknesses for each.
- Hidden assumptions.
- Smallest useful test, if relevant.
